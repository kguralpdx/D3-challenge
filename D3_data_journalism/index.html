Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Poverty vs. Lack of Healthcare

Based on the data in this scatter plot, it shows that there is a positive, linear relationship between poverty and and the lack of healthcare. Based on the states, it appears that the states with higher poverty levels and higher need for healthcare are found most often in the southern region of the US.

Age vs. Lack of Healthcare

After reviewing the data in the scatter plot, there appears to be a minimal but positive, somewhat linear relationship between the median age in a state and lack of healthcare. The dots are pretty spread out so the relationship between the two is weak. With the exception of some outliers, it does look like states with an older median age do have more healthcare coverage.

Age vs. Smoking

Based on the data in this scatter plot, there does appear to be a positive, linear relationship between a state's median age and the percentage of smokers. Utah, with the youngest population also seems to have the least number of smokers so hopefully that's a good sign for future generations of non-smokers. California and Texax, the two states with the biggest populations, are also on the lower end of the smoker percentage.

Poverty vs. Smoking

After analyzing the data in this scatter plot, there does appear to be a positive, linear relationship between poverty and the percentage of smokers. It's not a strong relationship as the dots are somewhat widespread. But the pattern is uphill so it's positive.
